Did Josh Dobbs get Benched? What happened to him?

Publish date: 2024-08-10

In a recent game against the Las Vegas Raiders, the Minnesota Vikings made a pivotal decision as quarterback Josh Dobbs found himself benched, making way for Nick Mullens to take the field. Dobbs struggled throughout the match, completing only 10 of 23 passes for 63 yards and enduring five sacks.

This turn of events follows Dobbs’ initial promise when he joined the Vikings in October, leading the team to two victories. However, his subsequent struggles raised concerns, culminating in his benching after a tough game against the Raiders.

The question now lingers on whether Mullens’ takeover is a temporary measure or if he will continue as the starting quarterback for the Vikings. The uncertainty adds an intriguing layer to the team’s dynamics.

Adding to the challenges faced by the Vikings in this game, star receiver Justin Jefferson exited early due to a chest injury. The combined impact of Dobbs’ benching and Jefferson’s absence raises questions about the team’s strategy moving forward and how they plan to navigate these hurdles in the upcoming matches.
