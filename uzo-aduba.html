Uzo Aduba- Biography

Uzo Aduba is a well-known actress from the United States. She is well recognized for her depiction of Suzanne’s “Crazy Eyes” Warren in the Netflix original series “Orange Is the New Black,” which is considered one of her breakthrough roles. She is also well-known for her supporting part in the drama film “American Pastoral,” starring Ewan McGregor and Jennifer Connelly. The professional actress is also recognized for being the only person to win an Emmy Award for both drama and comedy for the same role.

Uzo Aduba- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings, Education

Uzo, real name Uzoamaka Nwanneka “Uzo” Aduba, was born on February 10, 1981, in Boston, Massachusetts, USA. She is currently 40 years old. Uzo was born in Nigeria to Nigerian parents, as were her two brothers and sister, Obi Aduba, a professional hockey player, and Chioma Aduba. Aquarius is her astrological sign.

Uzo grew raised in Medfield, Massachusetts, and graduated from Medfield High School in 1999. She then enrolled at Boston University to study classical voice. Aduba used to compete in track and field at that school. She refers to her family as a “Sports Family” because they are all sports fans, and her brother is a well-known hockey player.

Uzo Aduba- Professional Career

Aduba rose to fame in 2003, when she appeared in “Translations of Xhosa” at the Olney Theatre Centre for the Arts. She began her climb to fame in 2006, when she starred as Amphiarus in a play at New York Theatre Workshop. She continued her career by working at La Jolla Playhouse in 2008. At the Imperial Theatre in 2007, she made her Broadway debut as Toby in Helen Edmundson’s production of Coram Boy. However, she sang “By My Side” in 2011 when playing as a member of the original revival cast of “Godspell” at the Circle in the Square Theatre until 2012. She made her debut television appearance as a nurse on “Blue Bloods” in 2012. She also won a role as the titular character’s mother in the New York-based drama Venice, which was produced at The Public Theatre.

Aduba never looked back in her career and began appearing in the new pole by portraying Suzanne “Crazy Eyes” Warren in the Netflix comedy-drama series “Orange Is the New Black” from 2013 to 2019, which was one of her most significant breakthrough roles. She also appeared as Glinda the Good Witch in the 2015 NBC live musical event special “The Wiz Live!” She then collaborated with Rachel Bay Jones on a rendition of “Lily’s Eyes,” a song from the musical album “The Secret Garden.”
