Peter Monn: Denies GoFundMe Donations Following Car Accident

Publish date: 2024-07-18

A week after the news of YouTuber Peter Monn’s car accident broke, Peter Monn took to Twitter to respond to the GoFundMes that had been set up for him and his husband.

Monn wrote that several people had started GoFundMe campaigns that raised between $500 and $1 million.

The YouTuber stated that, while he appreciated the efforts, he did not approve of these and requested that they be removed.

“If I ever needed money to beg for,” he wrote. “I’d do it myself.”

Monn followed up with a separate tweet an hour later, explaining why he wanted the GoFundMes to be removed.

“I wanted to address the Go Fund Me’s because the Go Fund Me’s by myself or my husband are not by us and we are not directly related to them,” Monn wrote.

“As a result, we are requesting that those directly stating they are raising funds for us be removed.”

On February 16, 2022, Monn was involved in a car accident in Fishers, Indiana. The car accident happened on Allisonville Road around 12:30 p.m., according to the police department’s statement.

The car accident involved three vehicles: a tan Chevrolet 1500 pickup truck, a gray Jeep Grand Cherokee, and a brown Nissan Altima.

Monn was the only one driving a Jeep Grand Cherokee. The driver of the Chevrolet was pronounced dead at the scene, one of the people involved in the car accident.

Monn and the driver of the Nissan Altima were both taken to the hospital.

Keemstar was the first to report on the unfortunate accident. He initially stated that the details were unknown, but later provided a screenshot of The Press Engine report, which revealed that Monn was one of the lucky survivors.

Peter Monn Update

Following the news of Monn’s car accident, rumors spread like wildfire on social media.

While the police stated that the accident was still under investigation, many netizens speculated that Monn was having a seizure at the time of the crash.

“According to people in his Vloggerino FB group who have contacted his husband, Alex, he had a seizure while driving,” one user wrote. He also suffers from epilepsy.”

However, nothing was confirmed, and the true cause of the accident is still unknown.

There was also little information about Monn’s situation until he tweeted about it himself.

“I woke up with no memory of 3 three, five staples in my head, head contusions, a broken back (which I’ll be in a brace for at least 6 months), a bruised lung, a broken rib, and cuts and scrapes all over,” he wrote.

Monn stated that he would return to creating content but did not know when. He also stated that he would “attend” social media whenever he felt like it.

Finally, the YouTuber expressed gratitude to everyone who had helped him and his husband, as well as those who had been injured in the accident.
