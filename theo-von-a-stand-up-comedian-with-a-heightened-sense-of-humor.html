Early Life and Family

Theodor Capitani von Kurnatowski was born on March 19, 1980, in Covington, Louisiana. Roland Theodor Achilles von Kurnatowski, his father, was a Polish-Nicaraguan businessman and philanthropist who owned various properties and enterprises, including New Orleans’ legendary Tipitina’s music venue.

Gina Capitani, his mother, is an Italian-American woman who has worked as a secretary and a hairdresser. Theo has two younger sisters and an older brother. When Theo’s father became pregnant with his mother, who was 32 years his junior, he was 68 years old.

In his stand-up routines, Theo frequently makes fun of his father’s age and unique upbringing. His father, he claims, was a loving and adventurous man who loved animals and flying planes, but he also had a temper and a drinking problem. He died of cancer when Theo was sixteen.

Theo grew up in a difficult neighborhood where he was bullied and encountered bigotry. He was officially emancipated at the age of 14 and began working as a busboy and landscaper. He also developed a drug addiction and a gambling addiction, both of which he eventually overcame.

Career and Achievements

Theo debuted in the entertainment industry at the age of 19 when he appeared on MTV’s Road Rules: Maximum Velocity Tour in 2000. Following that, he appeared in four seasons of MTV’s The Challenge, a reality game show starring competitors from The Real World and Road Rules.

He won two seasons and finished second in another. He also won the online competition on Season 4 of Last Comic Standing in 2006. Theo thereafter concentrated on his stand-up comedy, appearing in clubs and festivals across the country.

From 2011 to 2013, he hosted the Yahoo online TV recap show Primetime in No Time, and from 2013 to 2014, he hosted the TBS hidden camera show Deal With It. He also starred in Inside Amy Schumer, Why? with Hannibal Buress, and Sons of Anarchy, where he played a recurring character dubbed Lenny “The Pimp” Janowitz.

Theo is also well-known for his podcasts, which display his witty and candid attitude. He is the host of This Past Weekend, a podcast in which he and his guests and callers discuss their lives, current events, and diverse issues.

He also co-hosted The King and the Sting, a podcast in which he and fellow comic Brendan Schaub ridicule each other and their listeners. He’s also appeared on several notable podcasts, including The Joe Rogan Experience, The Fighter and the Kid, and Your Mom’s House.
